Founder

Petra Wennberg

Born in Sweden, based in Southern California. Cofounder of Interactivism, a digital product design studio in Pasadena. Human-centered designer, community builder, and mentor.

Extensive experience designing foundational products in Search, Social Media, AdTech, FinTech, and Blockchain — helped define and design the infrastructure that makes up the internet of today. Helped create more than $2B in exits.

Clients include NASA/JPL, Idealab, Caltech, and Intel's Incubation & Disruptive Innovation Group. Mentor for Summit Impact. Founding Executive Board Member of Innovate Pasadena (in collaboration with JPL, Caltech, ArtCenter College of Design, and the City of Pasadena).

After the Eaton Fire devastated her Altadena neighborhood in January 2025, Petra began applying everything she had learned about human-centered design to the question that mattered most: how do communities govern themselves when the systems they relied on have failed?

Alting is the answer she is building.

Research Foundations

Built on what already works.

Alting draws on decades of research and centuries of practice in how people govern shared resources, collaborate across difference, and build trust.

Elinor Ostrom

Nobel Prize in Economic Sciences, 2009. Eight principles for managing shared resources without privatization or top-down control. The theoretical backbone of every Ting.

Scrum

Iterative, time-boxed collaboration with clear roles and accountability. The operational rhythm that makes Ting governance practical, not just aspirational.

Swedish Associations

COOP, Friskis&Svettis, and similar structures where members govern and benefit equally. The cultural DNA of one-person-one-vote, practiced at national scale for over a century.

ROSCAs

Rotating and Accumulating Savings Clubs — Stokvels, Tandas, Partnerhand, Susu. Trust-based, peer-to-peer financial cooperation used by billions worldwide. 95% women participants.

Feminist Foreign Policy

Sweden's Margot Wallström pioneered Rights, Representation, Resources as governance principles. Applied here as a framework for inclusive, accountable community governance.

DAOs

Decentralized autonomous organizations for transparent collective governance. The technological precedent for blockchain-recorded, community-owned decision-making.

Values

What we stand for — and what we refuse.

We commit to

  • Transparency — all decisions visible, all funds traceable
  • Subsidiarity — decisions at the lowest possible level
  • Inclusion — voice tied to participation, not wealth
  • Circulation — value stays local, benefits the community
  • Protection — vulnerable populations prioritized
  • Long-term thinking — sustainable over expedient
  • Humility — we learn as we go; no one has all the answers

We reject

  • Power concentration — it creates exploitation
  • Wealth-weighted governance — it silences the majority
  • Value extraction — it drains communities
  • Opacity — it enables corruption
  • Displacement — it destroys social fabric
  • Speculation on disaster — it profits from pain
  • Technology without ethics — tools without purpose

Global Alignment

UN Sustainable Development Goals

Alting's framework aligns with 13 of 17 UN Sustainable Development Goals: No Poverty, Good Health & Well-being, Quality Education, Gender Equality, Decent Work & Economic Growth, Industry Innovation & Infrastructure, Reduced Inequalities, Sustainable Cities & Communities, Responsible Consumption & Production, Climate Action, Life on Land, Peace Justice & Strong Institutions, and Partnerships for the Goals.

This alignment is not aspirational — it is structural. Each component of Alting maps directly to specific SDG targets through its design, not through add-on programs.

What is a Ting?

A Ting is any organization people govern together — a block group, a water company, a land trust, a business corridor. Members participate as equals, make decisions transparently, and build trust through structured collaboration.
