How It Works

Alting is built on five components. Each serves a distinct function. Together they form a complete infrastructure for community self-governance.

Five Components

1

Sovereign Digital ID (Digital DNA)

Given at birth, owned by the individual, post-quantum encrypted. It holds your identity, data, credentials, and money. You decide what to share, with whom, and for how long. No platform, corporation, or government controls it.

Core principle: consensus is tied to verified personhood, not wallets or tokens. You cannot buy more voice.

2

Framework for Collaborations (Ting)

A templated toolkit for trust-based collaboration, grounded in Elinor Ostrom's principles for managing shared resources, Scrum's iterative structure, the trust mechanics of rotating savings clubs used by billions worldwide, and the democratic traditions of Swedish member-driven associations.

Core principle: where current systems measure spend, clicks, and engagement, Tings measure belonging, trust, safety, and financial security.

3

Democratic Governance

One person, one vote. Scalable from a block group to a city council. All decisions recorded on blockchain. Transparent, self-governed, accountable. The ability to elect Council members for larger Tings ensures the system scales without losing its democratic foundation.

Core principle: quantum-encrypted election mechanics enable safe digital voting at every scale.

4

Education (Learning Weave)

A modular platform covering financial literacy, governance, health, and emotional intelligence — taught by mentors with high trust signal, in collaboration with educational institutions. Some Tings require completing certain modules before participation, ensuring members are equipped to collaborate effectively.

Core principle: certifications are stored in your Digital DNA — learning is yours to keep.

5

Federation (Alting Layer)

The layer that connects all Tings into a unified network. It enables a bottom-up marketplace, collective bargaining for affordable services, decentralized voting infrastructure, and a testbed for quantum modelling of resource allocation and policy-making.

Core principle: a network of emotionally intelligent, collaborative members acts as a stabilizing partner to current economies.

The Bottom-Up Sequence

Every layer exists to serve the one below it.

Alting does not start from a platform and add users. It starts from a person and builds outward — neighbor to block, block to Ting, Ting to federation.

Person A person proves who they are. Sovereign identity is the seed.
Household Individuals form a household unit within their community.
Block Neighbors on a block begin making decisions together.
Zone Ting Blocks form a zone — the first formal governance body.
Federation Multiple Tings federate for cross-community coordination.
Alting Foundation Provides shared infrastructure only. Cannot override any Ting's decisions.

Each circle must be complete before the next one begins — not perfect, but functional. A working loop, not a partial feature set.

What Alting Measures

Different metrics for a different purpose.

Alting measures

  • Belonging
  • Trust
  • Safety
  • Financial security
  • Emotional intelligence
  • Environmental stewardship
  • Stronger local economies
  • Engagement in local governance
  • Strengthened democratic values

Alting does not measure

  • Engagement
  • Clicks
  • Spend
  • Growth
  • Virality
  • Time on platform
  • Conversion rates
  • User acquisition
  • Revenue per user

What is a Ting?

A Ting is any organization people govern together — a block group, a water company, a land trust, a business corridor. Members participate as equals, make decisions transparently, and build trust through structured collaboration.

Alting — Petra Wennberg. Altadena, California.
