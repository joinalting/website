Alting is infrastructure for community self-governance.

The systems we depend on were not designed to benefit everyone. They optimize for extraction — spend, engagement, clicks, accumulation. The result is widening inequality, eroding trust, and an epidemic of disconnection.

Alting is not a platform. It is infrastructure — a framework for self-governed, trust-based, democratic collaboration. Not owned. Not extracted from. Built to be governed by the people who use it.

The basic unit is the Ting: any organization people govern together. A neighborhood block group. A water company. A housing cooperative. A local government. The pattern is always the same — members participate as equals and build trust through structured collaboration.
